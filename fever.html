Note:

Use these medicines twice a day after having food for three days or less, otherwise consult doctor.

Food to be taken:

  • Hydrating Foods:
    • Consume water-rich fruits and vegetables such as watermelon, cucumber, oranges, and berries to stay well-hydrated. Adequate hydration is crucial during a fever.
  • Broth-based Soups:
    • Chicken or vegetable broth-based soups are not only hydrating but also provide essential nutrients. The warmth can be soothing, and the broth is easy on the stomach.
  • Plain Crackers or Toast:
    • Simple carbohydrates like plain crackers or toast can be easy to digest and provide a source of energy.
  • Bananas:
    • Bananas are gentle on the stomach and provide potassium, an electrolyte that may be depleted during fever and sweating.
  • Oatmeal or Rice Porridge:
    • These bland and easily digestible options can provide a good source of energy and are gentle on the stomach.
